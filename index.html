About Us

Welfare for Stray Dogs is a non-profit trust dedicated to protecting and improving the lives of Mumbai's street dogs. Since 1985, we have been working tirelessly to provide rescue, medical care, sterilization, and vaccination programs that create a safer environment for both dogs and people.

Our mission began with stopping the mass culling of street dogs, and today, we continue to advocate for humane and sustainable solutions. What We Do
Mass Sterilisation

Mass
Sterilisation

Our sterilisation program, based in a Mumbai Municipal Corporation centre, follows WHO and Animal Welfare Board guidelines. BMC staff, our team, and volunteers bring in street dogs for surgery. Surgeons sterilise and notch ears for ID, vaccinating each dog before release after a 7-day recovery. Citizen involvement helps reduce human-canine conflict, with visible impact from Cuffe Parade to Mahim/Sion.
